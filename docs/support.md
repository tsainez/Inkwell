# Inkstone Support

Inkstone is an iPad app for practicing Chinese (Hanzi) and Japanese (Kanji)
character handwriting with Apple Pencil. If something isn't working, you have
a question, or you'd like to suggest a feature, we're happy to help.

## Contact

- **Email:** [tsainez@gmail.com](mailto:tsainez@gmail.com) — for questions,
  problems, and feedback. We aim to reply within a few business days.
- **Bug reports & feature requests:**
  [GitHub Issues](https://github.com/tsainez/Inkwell/issues) — if you have a
  GitHub account, filing an issue is the fastest way to track a fix.

When reporting a problem, please include:

1. Your iPadOS version (Settings → General → About on your iPad).
2. The app version (Inkstone → Settings → About).
3. What you were doing and what you expected to happen instead. If a specific
   character misbehaves, tell us which one.

## Frequently asked questions

### Why was my stroke marked wrong?

Inkstone checks three things about every stroke: **order** (which stroke of
the character it is), **direction** (e.g. left-to-right, not right-to-left),
and **shape**. A red mark means one of those didn't match the reference form.
If grading feels too harsh while you're learning, switch **Settings →
Grading** to **Lenient**.

### Do I need an Apple Pencil?

No. An Apple Pencil gives the best feel, but you can practice with your
finger — the canvas accepts any input.

### Does Inkstone need an internet connection?

No. All stroke reference data for thousands of characters ships inside the
app. Everything works fully offline, always.

### Which characters can I practice?

The built-in decks cover JLPT N5 kanji, HSK 1 essentials, and shared
numbers — and you can type in any Chinese or Japanese character from the
Library screen to practice exactly what you're studying.

### How do I reset my progress?

**Settings → Data → Reset all progress** erases all practice history from
your iPad. Deleting the app does the same. Nothing is stored anywhere else.

### Where does my data go?

Nowhere. Inkstone has no accounts, no analytics, and never connects to a
server — see the [privacy policy](../privacy/).
